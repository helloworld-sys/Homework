Clove Hitch

Reasons To Use:

This knot in particular is used to secure a boat when docking. It is most effectively used to secure a middle section of rope to an object it crosses over, such as a line on a fencepost. It can also be used as an ordinary hitch, or as a binding knot, but it is not particularly secure in either application.


How To: Clove Hitch

  1. Hold the rope in your hand and identify the standing end (the longer part of the rope that is not actively being used to tie the knot).
  2. Pass the standing end of the rope around the object (such as a pole or another rope) from the back to the front. Cross the standing end over the top of the working end.
  3. Take the working end and cross it over the standing end. This will create an X shape over the object.
  4. Pass the working end behind the object again, crossing over the standing end once more. This creates a second loop. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends simultaneously. The result should be a secure clove hitch.
